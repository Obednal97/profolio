## 7. Your Data Protection Rights

Under GDPR and similar laws, you have the following rights:

### 7.1 Right of Access (Art. 15 GDPR)

- Request a copy of your personal data
- Information about how we process your data
- Details about data sharing and retention
- **Response Time:** 30 days maximum

### 7.2 Right to Rectification (Art. 16 GDPR)

- Correct inaccurate personal data
- Complete incomplete data records
- Update outdated information
- **Implementation:** Immediate for urgent corrections

### 7.3 Right to Erasure (Art. 17 GDPR)

- Request deletion of your personal data
- "Right to be forgotten" in certain circumstances
- Account closure and data purging
- **Note:** Some data may be retained for legal obligations

### 7.4 Right to Restrict Processing (Art. 18 GDPR)

- Limit how we process your data
- Temporary suspension of certain activities
- Marking data for restricted use
- **Duration:** Until resolution of underlying issue

### 7.5 Right to Data Portability (Art. 20 GDPR)

- Receive your data in structured, machine-readable format
- Transfer data to another service provider
- Export portfolio and transaction data
- **Format:** JSON, CSV, or other standard formats

### 7.6 Right to Object (Art. 21 GDPR)

- Object to processing based on legitimate interests
- Opt out of direct marketing
- Stop automated decision-making
- **Implementation:** Within 30 days

### 7.7 Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making

- Right not to be subject to purely automated decisions
- Request human review of automated decisions
- Challenge algorithmic recommendations
- **Application:** Portfolio recommendations, risk assessments

## 9. Data Security Measures

### 9.1 Technical Safeguards

- **Encryption:** AES-256 for data at rest and in transit
- **Access Controls:** Multi-factor authentication and role-based access
- **Network Security:** Firewalls, intrusion detection, VPN access
- **Monitoring:** 24/7 security monitoring and alerting

### 9.2 Organizational Measures

- **Staff Training:** Regular data protection training
- **Access Management:** Principle of least privilege
- **Incident Response:** Documented breach response procedures
- **Vendor Management:** Due diligence and contractual protections

### 9.3 Data Minimization

- Collect only necessary data
- Regular data audits and cleanup
- Automated retention policy enforcement
- Purpose limitation and use restrictions

## 10. Data Retention

### 10.1 Retention Principles

- **Necessity:** Retain only as long as needed
- **Proportionality:** Balanced against data subject rights
- **Legal Requirements:** Comply with statutory retention periods
- **Business Needs:** Legitimate operational requirements

### 10.2 Retention Periods

| Data Category | Retention Period | Legal Basis |
|---------------|------------------|-------------|
| Account Data | Account lifetime + 7 years | Legal obligation |
| Financial Records | 7 years after account closure | Regulatory requirement |
| Marketing Data | Until consent withdrawal | Consent management |
| Security Logs | 5 years | Legitimate interests |
| Analytics Data | 2 years (anonymized thereafter) | Legitimate interests |

### 10.3 Disposal Procedures

- **Secure Deletion:** Cryptographic erasure and overwriting
- **Physical Media:** Certified destruction services
- **Backups:** Systematic purging from all backup systems
- **Documentation:** Audit trail of disposal activities

## 11. Data Breach Management

### 11.1 Incident Response

**Detection and Assessment:**
- Automated monitoring and alerting
- Risk assessment and impact analysis
- Classification of breach severity
- Containment and mitigation measures

**Notification Timeline:**
- **Supervisory Authority:** Within 72 hours (if high risk)
- **Data Subjects:** Without undue delay (if high risk)
- **Internal Reporting:** Immediate escalation procedures

### 11.2 Breach Types and Responses

**Personal Data Breaches:**
- Unauthorized access or disclosure
- Accidental loss or destruction
- System compromise or cyber attacks
- **Response:** Full investigation, notification, remediation

**Technical Breaches:**
- System outages or failures
- Data corruption or unavailability
- Service interruptions
- **Response:** Technical resolution, impact assessment
